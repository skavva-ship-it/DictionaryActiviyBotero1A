Acquiesce (ak-wee-ESS)

Definition: To Acquiesce is to accept, agree, or allow something to happen by staying silent or by not arguing


Sentence 1: "As the long arugment comes to an end, Alice acquiesces to give the magic teapot what it wants"


Sentence 2: "I acquiesced to agree to the teacher's demands"


Synonym: Agree, Assume, Allow

Antonym: Frown, Forbid

Rally (Raah-lee)

Rally: To come together again in order to continue fighting after a defeat or dispersion.


Sentence 1: "The commander rallied the troops to encourage them"


Sentence 2: "Rallying the troops seemed to help the math team to win the competition"


Synonym: Gather, Assemnble, Regroup

Ratify (Ra-tiff-fye)

Ratify: To sign or give formal consent to (a treaty, contract, or agreement), making it officially valid.


Sentence 1: "The Japanese and the US ratified a peace treaty after the war."


Sentence 2: "If the two countries were to ratify a treaty, the world would be at peace"


Synonym: Agree, Consent, Confirm

Antonym: Dissagree, Uncoordinated
